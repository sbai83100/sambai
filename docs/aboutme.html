Hi, I'm Sam Bai!

I'm a rising junior at the University of Maryland, College Park pursuing a bachelor's degree in Computer Science and Statistics. I'm always actively refining my programming skills and learning new technologies, as well as finding multiple ways to connect with people to engage in deep technical and social conversations.

Experience

LexisNexis Risk Solutions

Currently, I'm a Quality Test Engineer Intern @ LexisNexis Risk Solutions, working on scoring quality assurance initiatives related to analytic testing frameworks and the cloud journey. I'm creating a dynamic dashboard to track movement of attributes that shifted from the previous day and display information about these attributes. The goal is to increase the level of automation and efficiency involved in reporting changes within attribute distribution reports, reducing the amount of time it takes to analyze large-scale datasets.

Additionally, I shadow an Agile team of 8 members, partaking in daily Scum sessions and weekly grooming sessions to gain first-hand experience with Scrum processes and the Agile framework. We use Jira to track and manage current software development projects and frequently update tickets to release products.

Iribe Initiative for Inclusion and Diversity in Computing

On campus, I'm a Teaching Outreach Ambassador for the Iribe Initiative for Inclusion and Diversity in Computing and the Maryland Center for Women in Computing. Long names aside, I connect with over 700 students of traditionally underrepresented minorities in computer science to promote diversity in tech.

I prepare weekly technology-rich workshops in cybersecurity, UI/UX, and data science for over 100 total participants and design instructional programs in Python to assist over 30 students in developing programming skills and problem-solving crucial to the AP Computer Science curricula. In addition, I help host student panels targeted towards women and BIPOC, serving 439 unique participants and 64 total teacher participants.

Leadership and Student Involvement

Technica

Technica is the world's largest all-women, non-binary hosted annually at the University of Maryland, College Park. In 2020, we hosted an unprecedented virtual hackathon with over 1800 hackers from across the world.

As part of our initative to extend our mission across the globe, Technica is looking to expand to the west coast, partnering with schools in California to create a safe, diverse, and inclusive community for underrepresented genders in tech in the San Francisco Bay Area. As our West Coast Lead, I've reached out to over 10 schools and hackathons on the west coast to find a strategic partner that will assist us in hosting a simultaneous hackathon in Fall 2021.
