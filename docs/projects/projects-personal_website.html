Motivation

One of my very first work experiences was creating and designing a website for a client. Without much knowledge in HTML/CSS at the time, I took great advantage of WordPress's simple design templates and Bluehost's website hosting prowess.

Over quarantine, as I was browsing LinkedIn, I noticed how several connections had their own personal websites, showcasing their projects, photography, art, marketing promotions, etc. I was looking for a project to work on, so naturally, I decided to create my own personal website.

What you see here today is the product of multiple iterations, with each iteration more complex than the last. Bluehost requires you to purchase a subscription plan, which I was not at liberty to do. I had to find a way to create and host a website without spending a penny.

The first and simplest iteration was just creating a website through Wix. Although it accomplished the job, I realized how restricting and limiting their design templates were. Beyond changing color schemes and block layouts, customization was quite lackluster.
